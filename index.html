Think of inspections & reports

Answer · Quality Control UK System
The UK monitors quality through:
  • Regular Ofsted inspections (and equivalent bodies in devolved regions).
  • Public performance reports that make school results transparent.
  • These tools keep schools accountable and push them to maintain consistent standards.

Think: rural quality & dropouts
Front · Question
Answer · Remaining Gaps Nepal System
Key challenges are:
  • A strong quality gap between urban and rural schools.
  • Insufficient teacher training and limited learning resources in many areas.
  • Still-high dropout rates, especially at the secondary level.

Q9 · Nepal Learning from UK Slide 10
According to your study, what three practices from the UK could help Nepal strengthen its education system?
Hint: inspections, vocational paths & data
Front · Question
Answer · Lessons for Nepal Cross-Learning
Nepal can learn from the UK by:
  • Creating inspection systems like Ofsted to enforce accountability.
  • Expanding vocational education linked directly to real jobs.
  • Using data-driven planning to track learning outcomes and allocate resources effectively.

Q10 · UK Learning from Nepal Slide 11–12
The exchange is two-way. What can the UK learn from Nepal to move closer to SDG 4?
Think: community & inclusivity
Front · Question
Answer · Lessons for the UK Cross-Learning
From Nepal, the UK can learn to:
  • Strengthen community engagement and local ownership of schools.
  • Use low-cost, flexible learning tools such as radio or offline digital lessons for underserved groups.
  • Deepen its focus on inclusivity and cultural diversity in a multicultural context.
